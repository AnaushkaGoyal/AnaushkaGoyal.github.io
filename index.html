About

I am a Master of City Planning student at the University of Pennsylvania, concentrating in Urban Spatial Analytics. My work focuses on analyzing how land use, housing, transit, and environmental risk interact within cities. I approach planning problems through spatial data and quantitative analysis rather than description alone.

I work with Python, R, QGIS, ArcGIS, and SQL to produce applied outputs such as land use diagnostics, zoning comparisons, environmental risk overlays, growth forecasts, and interactive dashboards. My emphasis is on structuring data clearly and linking analysis to planning or development decisions.

Before graduate school, I worked in urban research and design in Mumbai, supporting open space studies and community-based planning projects. That experience strengthened my ability to work with incomplete datasets, regulatory documents, and stakeholder constraints while keeping analysis grounded in implementation.

I am seeking roles where spatial analysis supports real decisions across industries, and I am particularly interested in work where technical analysis is directly tied to strategy and execution.
